On September 15, 2020, the leaders of the UAE and Israel signed the historic accords at the White House, fully normalizing relations between the two countries.

The UAE is a leading trade, transport, and financial hub in the Middle East, while Israel, known as the "Start-Up Nation," excels in technology and innovation with the highest per capita startups, R&D spending, and tech unicorns.

The UAE and Israel are open, dynamic, and multi-cultural societies, which promote religious tolerance, celebrate innovation, and seek to better the lives of their citizens.

The UAE was established in 1971 and Israel in 1948, yet in the short time since their independence, they have developed into the most socially, economically and educationally advanced nations in the region.
